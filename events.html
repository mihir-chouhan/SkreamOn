Rules
1. No. of players per team: 5+2
2. Rolling substitutions are allowed
3. Half length is of 7 minutes The rules n regulations for rink football are as follows:-
1) The tournament will proceed on a knockout basis.
2) Two halves of 7 mins each with 1 minute break between them.
3) Tie will be decided by end to end penalties( best of three)
4) Throw-ins will be replaced by touch-ins.
5) 5 straight corners conceded are penalized with 1 end to end penalty.
6) High passes are allowed in your own half but across the field passes have to be below chest height.
7) Free kicks can be direct in the attacking half. However, in the team's own half they have to be indirect and the rise rule is applicable once again.
8) Rolling substitutions are allowed.
9) Hand balls and fowls will be penalized with free kick or penalty depending on the position.
10) Squad of 6- 4 on field players and 2 substitutes.

BOX CRICKET
  • Contact
  • General
Rules
1.No. of players per team: 6+2
2.Five overs a side
3.Minimum 4 bowlers(2-1-1-1)
4.1 tip 1 hand out.
5.Direct boundary cross out.
6.4 runs nd 2 runs declared will be marked.
7.No juggling while taking a catch after 1 tip.
8.6 players per team, 2 substitute.
9.Wide / No ball - 2 runs.
10.No ball criteria: (Above Waist height) 2nd bounce is no ball and direct (full toss) above knee level is no ball.
11.Wide + stumping = wkt +1 run(Ball not counted)
12.If ball crosses the boundaries directly after touching a player, runs will be given accordingly. The batsmen will be not-out.
13.On the fall of every wicket, 3 runs will be deducted from the score each time.

14.Challenge Over : The batting team has to select an over. The batting team will then tell the umpire the number of runs they have set as a target for that over. If they achieve it, the no. Of runs that they have set for that over, will double. If they fail, they will be given no runs for that over meaning, it will be counted as a maiden over. If the batting team doesn't select a challenge over, then by default last over will be considered as challenge over. (Eg. If the batting team selects the 2nd over as the challenge over, and sets a target of 10 runs for that over. If they score 10, they will get 20 runs. If they fail they'll get 0)
